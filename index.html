I downloaded the Lato front from Google Fonts, inserted it with a style link in the header, and changed the color. I used the a tag to add a few hyperlinks to other sites. Finally I used another div tag to put the whole site contents in a dotted box. This was fun! I had never really read any Twain outside of Huckleberry Finn in school, but last year I read A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court on a whim and thoroughly enjoyed it. The titular Yankee gets bumped on the head, wakes up in early medieval England, and promtply begins re-inventing 19th century society. You can read the whole book right here, courtesy of the Internet Archive.
I was struck by the Yankee's use of the phrase "new deal" during a monologue in which he decries the opressed condition of the masses of serfs and imagines how he might instigate democracy (scroll to page 108 if you want to see for yourself). Turns out FDR was a big Twain fan and took some inspiration from the book. Twain's cousin Cyril Clemens recalls the following conversation with the President:
"Did you know, Mr. President, that the slogan of your administration was used by Twain in the Connecticut Yankee?" "Yes, certainly I was aware of that fact," he smilingly answered, "for it was there that I obtained the phrase. You recall the Yankee's saying that he was in a country where a right to say how the country should be governed was restricted to six persons in each thousand of its population...I felt the same way about conditions in America."
Shout out to user Calgacus on livingnewdeal.org for digging that up.
